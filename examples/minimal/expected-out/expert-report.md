# Expert report — Espresso brewing

Reconstructed 65 facts across 6 domains. Truth ledger: PLAUSIBLE 10 · TRUTH 46 · DISPUTED 8 · NEEDS-VERIFICATION 1.

## beans (10 facts · 5 TRUTH)

- Beans should rest at least 7 days after roasting before use
- Beans should be used within one month of roasting
- Optimal espresso window is 7 to 21 days after roasting
- Beans before 7 days post-roast are too gassy for optimal espresso
- Beans after 21 days post-roast show flavor degradation

## extraction (27 facts · 20 TRUTH)

- Sour shot indicates under-extraction
- Grinding finer increases extraction yield
- Raising water temperature increases extraction yield
- Bitter shot indicates over-extraction
- Grinding coarser decreases extraction yield
- Lowering water temperature decreases extraction yield

## grind (7 facts · 6 TRUTH)

- Finer grind size increases extraction yield by raising the coffee surface area exposed to water
- Finer grind size increases flow resistance, which raises brew pressure and slows the shot
- If the shot is sour, grind finer
- Tamping pressure is overrated; a level, evenly distributed bed is what actually prevents channeling
- Distribution matters more than tamping pressure; level the bed with a light tap, then tamp with consistent, moderate force
- If the shot tastes sour, grind finer and keep everything else fixed, because the grind is too coarse

## machine (10 facts · 6 TRUTH)

- Pump targets approximately 9 bar of brew pressure at the puck for classic espresso
- Pre-infusion applies low pressure first to wet the puck evenly, reducing channeling before full pressure is applied
- Purge the group head before every shot for temperature stability
- Purge the group head for one to two seconds before locking in the portafilter to clear old grounds and stabilize temperature
- Backflush the group with detergent once a week on a busy bar because rancid oils in the group ruin every shot that passes through it
- Clean the group head gasket weekly because old coffee oils are the most common cause of off-flavors people blame on beans

## milk (3 facts · 2 TRUTH)

- Milk steaming for espresso involves two sequential phases: air introduction (stretching) followed by heating and folding to build glossy microfoam
- Milk heated above approximately 65°C becomes scalded, causing protein breakdown and destruction of microfoam structure and sweetness

## water (8 facts · 7 TRUTH)

- Higher water temperature increases extraction yield, especially of slower-dissolving bitter compounds
- Water hardness increases extraction of flavor compounds
- Optimal water hardness for espresso is 50-80 ppm
- Very soft water produces flat espresso
- Very hard water causes boiler scaling
- Scale formation is the leading single cause of espresso machine failure
