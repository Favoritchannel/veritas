# Interview — Maria (head barista, 12 years)

Maria: For a double espresso I always dose 18 grams. That basket size is the sweet spot — enough body, no channeling.

Maria: Target yield is a 1:2 ratio, so 18 grams in should give you about 36 grams out.

Maria: Extraction time for a balanced double is 25 to 30 seconds, measured from the first drop.

Maria: Brew temperature for medium roasts should be 92 degrees Celsius. Hotter than that and you pull bitterness out of a medium roast.

Maria: If the shot tastes sour, your grind is too coarse — grind finer and keep everything else fixed.

Maria: Always purge the group head for a second or two before locking in the portafilter. It clears old grounds and stabilizes temperature.

Maria: Distribution matters more than tamping pressure. Level the bed with a light tap, then tamp with consistent, moderate force.

Maria: Fresh beans matter: use them between 7 and 21 days after roast. Before day 7 they are too gassy, after three weeks they go flat.

Maria: Water hardness around 50 to 80 ppm works best. Very soft water makes flat espresso, very hard water scales the boiler.

Maria: Clean the group head gasket weekly. Old coffee oils are the most common cause of off flavors people blame on beans.
