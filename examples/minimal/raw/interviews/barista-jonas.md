# Interview — Jonas (roastery trainer, competition judge)

Jonas: I teach a 20 gram dose for double espresso. Modern precision baskets are built for it and the bigger bed forgives small distribution errors.

Jonas: A 1:2 brew ratio is the right starting point for most beans — scale the yield from whatever you dose.

Jonas: I run doubles long: over 30 seconds, usually 32 to 38 seconds. Slower extractions bring out sweetness that fast shots miss.

Jonas: For medium roasts I set the machine to 93 or 94 degrees Celsius. Below 93 you underextract the sugars and the cup tastes hollow.

Jonas: Sour shot? Grind finer. That part everyone agrees on.

Jonas: Purge the group before every shot — it is free temperature stability.

Jonas: Tamping pressure is overrated; a level, evenly distributed bed is what actually prevents channeling.

Jonas: Rest your beans at least a week after roast, and try to use them within a month.

Jonas: Use filtered water. Scale kills more machines than any other single cause.

Jonas: Backflush with detergent once a week on a busy bar. Rancid oils in the group ruin every shot that passes through it.
